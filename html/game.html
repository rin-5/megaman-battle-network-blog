The games

The games, known worldwide as Mega Man Battle Network, are a unique mix of a real-time action RPG, turn-based JRPG and a card game akin to Yu-Gi-Oh or the Pok√©mon TCG, in which you can roam the world and send your Navi to the internet. And when you fight against another Navi or a virus, you can use your weapon and with the Custom Screen send Battle Chips (the card-collecting part) for your Navi to use! It even had online play (through the GBA Link cable and with the Legacy Collection through real internet). The main themes are friendship, bonds and how technology, when misused, can be very dangerous. It had six mainline games and two spinoffs. Toys and Peripherals

The games also had several toys and peripherals to use your toys with the games. Globally, only the first Advanced Pet released as a toy, but in Japan and Korea, all PETs from the Plug-In PET forwards released as toys and with Battle Chips. Though the chips could be used with the toys, from Battle Network 4 onwards, a series of devices that could connect to your Game Boy Advance were released by Capcom and Takara, the toymakers. These are the Battle Chip Gates: by connecting one to your console, you can send Chips in the middle of the battle to your navi! Some games, like the japan-exclusive Rockman.EXE 4.5 Real Operation were specifically made with this in mind. There are three models: The original one works with Battle Network 4, 4.5 and Mega Man Zero 3. Then there is the Progress Chip Gate, which works exclusively with Battle Network 5, and then the Beast Link Gate, which works with the sixth entry in the series.
